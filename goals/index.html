Goals of the Project

The ball is round, the game lasts 90 minutes. Everything else is pure theory.

Sepp Herberger

Football, or soccer, is a team sport popular among most parts of the world, attracting both amateur players and audience who donâ€™t even play the sport. Professional matches from leagues such as England Premier League are broadcasted to over 200 countries around the world, with millions of people watching at the same time.

The great number of audience is making it not only a sport but also a high profile business for bookmakers. People are fascinated in predicting the actual results of matches and betting for it. However, because of the variability, people make wrong predictions all the time.

We are wondering how good can we predict the results with historical data on two teams. Is there anything reasonable and predictable behind the odds? Can we do better than our intuition? With those questions, we want to build a prediction system that can predict the match result given the historical match data on those two teams.

Lucen Zhao, Zhihao Zhou, Jiayuan Xu, Siang Gao @CMU, 2018
